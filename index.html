My Story

It happened on a normal school day. I didn’t expect anything serious when I was called out of class. I just thought maybe it was a warning or some small issue to sort out.

But when I got to the office, everything changed. They told me I was involved in malpractice, and the decision had already been made. I was expelled.

At first, it didn’t even feel real. I just sat there trying to process what was being said. It felt like I was hearing about someone else, not me. When I left school that day, everything outside was still normal — people laughing, still in class, still living their day — but mine felt like it had stopped completely.

In the short term, I was just in shock. I kept replaying everything in my head, trying to figure out where it went wrong or what I could’ve done differently. I didn’t really want to talk to people about it because I felt embarrassed and honestly just tired of thinking about it. Even simple things like studying or planning for the future felt useless for a while.

I also started isolating myself a bit. Not because I wanted to, but because I didn’t know how to explain it to people without feeling judged. It was just this constant feeling of regret and confusion.

Long term, it changed a lot more than I expected. It affected my academic path and made me realise how quickly one decision can affect everything you’ve been working toward. I also saw how people react differently to you after something like that happens — some distance themselves, some stay the same.

But it also forced me to think differently. As hard as it was, it made me more aware of consequences and how important my choices are. I can’t just act in the moment anymore without thinking about what it could lead to.

Now, my focus is on changing. I don’t want this to define me or be the thing that decides my future. I’m trying to rebuild step by step — getting back into discipline, being more intentional with my decisions, and focusing on my academics again properly. I want to prove to myself more than anyone else that I can come back from this and do better.
